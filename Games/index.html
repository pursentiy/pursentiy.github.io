Some words about me and not just that

Hi! This page is all about gaming projects. The main purpose of these courses was not just to follow teachers‘s instructions but to get the knowledge and use this knowledge in various challenges either made by them or by my own. Besides, I learnt how to write nice and clean code and how to make them readable, simple, and efficient at the same time; although there is so much to learn and discover, I’ve already covered a lot, and I‘m really happy about it!
I have completed a bachelor’s degree in cybernetics; and now I am getting a master’s degree at the Moscow Technical University (MIREA).
Games have always been my passion. The older I got, the more I was involved in both playing and learning. My first experience with game engine was back in 2015, when I was learning C# at university. Although it was mostly for pleasure, I was eager to learn more. Later, I decided to dedicate myself to Web. Starting with simple pages and ending with more complex JavasScript based sites. After some time, I moved on and started my journey with Node.js, which was the topic of my bachelor thesis. Looking further to develop my skills, I took an internship in Slovakia at Ness (Digital Engineering). This was not only a major boost for my code writing experience, teamwork and problem-solving skills. I also gained an international perspective. During this time, I was writing an app, learning the basics of React and working on C# server, but meanwhile all that time I was reading a lot about games and this filed.
However, in the end I realized that Web is not what I really wanted, I was looking for something more creative, more alive. Without wasting time, I started, as I was thinking all the time about it, learning about game engines, I knew that it would be a tricky route, but I knew that it was worth all the efforts. After 8 months of studying, I decided to make another step: I applied for a job. At Komeata.games I began with a simple tasks: implementing game assets into scenes, VFX: particles, shaders etc. With time, I started coding and performing more diverse and complex tasks. And I feel that it's really what I like a lot!
